# Guidelines on Marketing Professional Services by Professional Accountants in Public Practice (2023)

*Issued by The Institute of Chartered Accountants of Nepal (ICAN)*

**Effective Date:** 17 July 2023

---

## 1. Objectives

Marketing of professional services provided by professional accountants in public practice is primarily intended to communicate with clients searching for accountants with adequate qualifications, experience, competence, and good repute.

However, such marketing, publicity, or advertisement may create a threat to compliance with the fundamental principles of the **Code of Ethics**. 
*   *Example:* A self-interest threat to professional behavior arises if services, achievements, or products are marketed in a way inconsistent with the principle.

Therefore, communication must not influence, persuade, or coerce clients through advertisement, publicity, lobbying, etc.

**Professional accountants in practice shall:**
*   Not bring the profession into disrepute when marketing services.
*   Be honest and truthful.
*   **NOT** make exaggerated claims for services, qualifications, or experience.
*   **NOT** make disparaging references or unsubstantiated comparisons to the work of others.

---

## 2. Marketing, Advertising, or Publicity

*   **Marketing:** A process to communicate with potential clients needing professional services. This may involve newspapers, magazines, signboards, television, radio, social media, websites, etc.
*   **Publicity:** Similar to advertisement but directed at potential or non-potential clients to make them aware that a professional accountant seeks clients.

**Key Restriction:**
The Code of Ethics (IFAC & ICAN) intends to restrain professional accountants from influencing, persuading, or coercing clients through exaggerated or unsubstantiated claims.

---

## 3. General Guidelines

### 3.1 Prohibited Communication
A Professional Accountant in Public Practice shall not seek clients through false, fraudulent, misleading, deceptive, or unfair means. Coercion, duress, compulsion, or harassment is strictly prohibited.

### 3.3 Public Interest Requirement
Public communication must not be self-serving but rather in the public interest. Prohibited advertising includes:
*   Guarantees of service inconsistent with regulatory decisions.
*   Unsubstantiated claims regarding costs or prices.
*   Unsubstantiated claims of professional superiority.
*   Offering inducements for services or products.

### 4.2 Publicity Guidelines

*   **Press Articles:** Permissible for educating the public on tax, audit, and financial matters.
*   **Sponsorship:**
    *   Sponsoring ICAN activities (simple banner allowed).
    *   Sponsoring charitable/social welfare events (simple banner allowed).
*   **Promotional Materials:** Diaries, calendars, etc., with the firm's name are allowed for distribution in the office.
*   **Announcements:** Paid announcements for opening new offices, changes in partnership, or address changes are permissible.
*   **Signposts:** Directional signposts to the office are allowed.
*   **Social Media:** Materials in the firm's or CA/RA's name are allowed, subject to Nepal Chartered Accountants Rules, 2061.

### 4.3 Professional Documents & Sign Boards

*   **Stationery:** Letterheads and visiting cards must conform to standards of professional good taste.
*   **Sign Board:**
    *   Allowed for the office (any reasonable size).
    *   **Prohibited:** Glow signs/lights (like shops).
    *   **Content:** Name and address only. Services mentioned must be limited to "Audit, Advisory, and Taxation". No other advertising content.
*   **Name Plate (Residence):** Allowed for individual members with designation.
*   **Logo:** Requires ICAN approval.
*   **Slogans:** Strictly prohibited on any stationery or display material.
*   **Greeting Cards:** Personal invitations should not indicate professional designation/status unless it's for a marriage, religious ceremony, or office inauguration.

### 4.4 Advertising

*   **Press:** Designation can be used in articles/letters of public interest.
*   **Media Appearances:** Members can appear on Radio/TV under their own name.
*   **Books:** Authorship of books/monographs is allowed, but association with a practicing firm cannot be indicated.
*   **Trade Associations:** Members holding office in trade associations must not use the position to acquire professional work.
*   **Staff Recruitment:** Advertisements for staff/articleship are allowed in non-professional press but must not be laudatory or promotional.

### 4.5 Solicitation

Soliciting work in any unprofessional manner is prohibited.
*   Former employees starting independent practice should not solicit clients of their former employer.
*   Members employed by a firm cannot undertake professional work on their own account without employer consent.

---

## 5. Prohibited Activities

Professional accountants in public practice are **strictly prohibited** from:
1.  Making exaggerated claims about services, qualifications, or experience in any communication (quotations, profiles, etc.).
2.  Making disparaging references or unsubstantiated comparisons to others.
3.  Allowing clients to use the auditor's name in documents other than financial statements.

---

## 6. In Case of Doubt

If a professional accountant is unsure whether a form of marketing is appropriate, they **must consult ICAN** prior to proceeding. Any marketing inconsistent with the Code of Ethics (IFAC & ICAN) is deemed illegal and liable for disciplinary action.

---

## 7. Supremacy of Code of Ethics

In case of conflict, the **Code of Ethics (IFAC & ICAN)** supersedes these guidelines.

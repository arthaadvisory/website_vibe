# Corporate Laws of Nepal

This guide serves as a comprehensive reference for the **Corporate Laws** syllabus (CAP-III), focusing on the **Nepal Chartered Accountants Act, 2053** and the **Companies Act, 2063**.

---

## Part A: Nepal Chartered Accountants Act, 2053

The Institute of Chartered Accountants of Nepal (ICAN) was established under this Act to regulate the accounting profession.

### 1. Constitution of the Council
The Council is the governing body of ICAN.
*   **Composition:** 17 Councilors (10 elected CAs, 4 elected RAs, 3 nominated by Govt).
*   **Tenure:** 3 Years.
*   **President/Vice-President:** Elected by Council members from among fellow CAs for a 1-year term.

### 2. Classes of Members
The Act classifies members into:
*   **Chartered Accountants (CA):** Passed CAP-III or equivalent.
*   **Registered Auditors (RA):** Holders of B, C, or D class certificates (provision for new RAs is restricted).

### 3. Certificate of Practice (COP)
No member can practice the profession of auditing without a valid COP.
*   **Disqualification:** Members convicted of criminal offenses, insolvent, or of unsound mind cannot hold a COP.
*   **Cancellation:** The Council can cancel a COP for misconduct or non-payment of fees.

::: tip Code of Conduct
Review the [ICAN Code of Ethics 2023](/resources/guides/ican-code-of-ethics-2023) for detailed ethical obligations of members.
:::

## Part B: Companies Act, 2063

The Companies Act governs the incorporation, management, and liquidation of companies in Nepal.

### 1. Incorporation of Company
*   **Private Company:** 1 to 101 shareholders. Cannot sell shares to the public. Name ends with "Pvt. Ltd."
*   **Public Company:** Minimum 7 promoters (no maximum). Can raise capital from the public. Name ends with "Ltd."
*   **Promoters:** Persons who sign the Memorandum of Association (MOA) and Articles of Association (AOA).

### 2. Memorandum & Articles (MOA/AOA)
*   **MOA:** The constitution of the company. Defines objectives, capital, and liability.
*   **AOA:** The bylaws. Defines internal management, meeting rules, and director powers.

### 3. Board of Directors
*   **Public Company:** Min 3, Max 11 directors. Must appoint at least one **Independent Director**.
*   **Private Company:** As per AOA (usually min 1).
*   **Disqualification (Sec 89):** Mental incapacity, insolvency, conviction of theft/fraud, or blacklisting by Credit Information Bureau.

### 4. Annual General Meeting (AGM)
*   **Requirement:** Every company must hold an AGM within **6 months** of the expiry of the fiscal year.
*   **First AGM:** Within 1 year of managing business commencement.
*   **Agenda:** Approval of accounts, appointment of auditor, election of directors, declaration of dividends.

### 5. Audit & Auditors
*   **Appointment:** Appointed by shareholders at the AGM.
*   **Term:** Until the next AGM.
*   **Cooling Off:** An auditor cannot be appointed for more than **3 consecutive terms** (for Public Companies).
*   **Rights:** Access to books of accounts at all times; Right to be heard at the AGM.

### 6. Voluntary Liquidation
A company can be voluntarily liquidated if:
1.  It is able to pay all debts in full.
2.  Shareholders pass a special resolution.
3.  Written declaration of solvency is filed by directors.

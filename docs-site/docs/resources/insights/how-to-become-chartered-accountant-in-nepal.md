Chartered Accountancy (CA) is one of the most prestigious and challenging professions in Nepal. Governed by **The Institute of Chartered Accountants of Nepal (ICAN)**, established under the Nepal Chartered Accountants Act, 1997, the CA profession offers immense career growth, respect, and financial stability.

Whether you are a student exploring career options or a graduate looking to switch fields, this guide covers everything you need to know about becoming a CA in Nepal.

## What is a Chartered Accountant?

Chartered Accountants (CAs) are trusted experts in auditing, accounting, financial reporting, taxation, corporate laws, and business advisory. They play a critical role in the economic development of the country, ensuring financial transparency and compliance for businesses, government bodies, and NGOs.

With the rapid growth of Nepal's economy, CAs are in high demand to hold top management positions in public and private sectors.

## The CA Education Structure

The CA education in Nepal is a **distance learning course** combined with rigorous practical training. It is divided into three levels:

### 1. CAP-I (Chartered Accountancy Professional-I)
*   **Level:** Entry / Foundation
*   **Duration:** 6 Months
*   **Eligibility:** 
    *   Completed 10+2, PCL, A-Level, or equivalent with a minimum of **2.4 GPA** or equivalent grades.
    *   Graduates/Post-Graduates with less than the minimum marks required for direct entry to CAP-II.
*   **Course Modules:**
    1.  Fundamentals of Accounting
    2.  Mercantile Laws & Fundamentals of Economics
    3.  Fundamentals of Management, Commercial Mathematics & Statistics

### 2. CAP-II (Chartered Accountancy Professional-II)
*   **Level:** Intermediate / Knowledge Level
*   **Duration:** 9 Months
*   **Eligibility:**
    *   Passed CAP-I Level.
    *   **Direct Entry for Graduates:** 
        *   Commerce/Management Graduates (Majoring in Accounts/Audit/Tax) with **50%** aggregate.
        *   Commerce/Management Graduates (Other majors) with **55%** aggregate.
        *   Non-Commerce Graduates with **60%** aggregate.
*   **Course Modules:**
    *   **Group I:** Advanced Accounting, Audit & Assurance, Corporate & Other Laws.
    *   **Group II:** Financial Management, Cost & Management Accounting, Business Communication & Marketing, Income Tax & VAT.

### 3. CAP-III (Chartered Accountancy Professional-III)
*   **Level:** Final / Expert Level
*   **Duration:** 3 Years (including Articleship Training)
*   **Eligibility:** Passed CAP-II Level and registered as an Article Trainee.
*   **Course Modules:**
    *   **Group I:** Advanced Financial Reporting, Advanced Financial Management, Advanced Auditing, Corporate Laws.
    *   **Group II:** Management Information & Control System, Advanced Taxation, Advanced Cost & Management Accounting, Strategic Management & Decision Making Analysis.

## Fee Structure & Costs

The fee structure for the CA course is quite affordable compared to other professional degrees.

| Level | Registration Fee |
| :--- | :--- |
| **CAP-I** | NPR 27,500 |
| **CAP-II** | NPR 32,500 |
| **CAP-III** | NPR 40,000 |
| **Total** | **NPR 1,00,000** |

*Note: Fees can be paid in full or in installments as per ICAN rules.*

## Practical Training (Articleship)

A unique feature of the CA course is the mandatory **3-year Articleship Training**. Students must work under a practicing Chartered Accountant to gain real-world experience in auditing, taxation, and consultancy.

During this period, students receive a monthly stipend:
*   **1st Year:** NPR 5,000/month
*   **2nd Year:** NPR 7,000/month
*   **3rd Year:** NPR 10,000/month

---

## Additional Training Requirements

Besides the academic exams and articleship, students must complete:
1.  **Information Technology (IT) Training:** 100 hours of mandatory IT training (60 hours before joining CAP-III and 40 hours during CAP-III).
2.  **General Management and Communication Skills (GMCS):** A 15-day course to enhance interpersonal and management skills.
3.  **Pre-Articleship Orientation:** A mandatory session for CAP-II passed students before starting their articleship.

## Scholarships and Facilities

### Scholarships
The Institute provides scholarships to needy and deserving students. 5% of the total registration fees collected are allocated to the Scholarship Fund. Special financial support is also available for students affected by natural disasters or pandemics.

### Library Facilities
Students have access to ICAN's library, which is stocked with professional books, guidance notes, and reference materials. An Identity Card is required to access these facilities.

### Extra-Curricular Activities
ICAN organizes various events like Elocution Contests, Quiz Contests, Debates, and International Conferences. Winners are often awarded medals and may represent Nepal in SAFA (South Asian Federation of Accountants) level programs.

## How to Enroll?

There is **no entrance examination** for the CA course. Eligible students can register online via the ICAN portal.

1.  **Visit:** [https://enroll.ican.org.np/](https://enroll.ican.org.np/)
2.  **Fill the Form:** Submit personal details and educational qualifications.
3.  **Upload Documents:** Self-attested copies of transcripts and certificates.
4.  **Payment:** Pay the registration fees online or via bank deposit.

---

## Career Scope of a CA

After qualifying, the career options are limitless. A Chartered Accountant can:
*   **Start Practice:** Open a CA firm and provide audit, tax, and advisory services (requires Certificate of Practice).
*   **Corporate Job:** Work as a CFO, Finance Manager, or Internal Auditor in banks, multinational companies, and improved private firms.
*   **Government Service:** Join government bodies or regulatory authorities in senior positions.
*   **Consulting:** Offer specialized services in mergers, acquisitions, and NFRS implementation.
*   **Global Opportunities:** With ICAN's membership in IFAC, CAPA, and SAFA, and MRAs with bodies like ICAEW (UK), CPA Australia, and ICAI (India), Nepali CAs have global recognition.

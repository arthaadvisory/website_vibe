# Director's Statutory Duty Checklist

As a Director of a company in Nepal, you bear significant legal responsibilities. Use this checklist to ensure you are meeting your statutory obligations under the *Companies Act, 2063* and preventing personal liability.

## 1. General Statutory Duties (Section 134)

- [ ] **Act Honestly:** I have acted honestly and in good faith for the benefit of the company.
- [ ] **No Personal Conflict:** I have not used my position to gain personal benefit or cause loss to the company.
- [ ] **Disclosure of Interest:** I have disclosed any personal interest in transactions entered into by the company.
- [ ] **Care and Diligence:** I have exercised the degree of care and diligence that a prudent person would exercise in similar circumstances.

## 2. Mandatory Filings & Meetings

- [ ] **Annual General Meeting (AGM):** Held within 6 months of the end of the fiscal year.
- [ ] **Annual Returns:** Filed with the Office of Company Registrar (OCR) within 30 days of the AGM.
- [ ] **Auditor Appointment:** Appointed a qualified auditor at the AGM and notified the OCR within 15 days.
- [ ] **Share Register:** Maintained an up-to-date register of shareholders and debenture holders.

## 3. Financial Integrity

- [ ] **Solvency:** ensured the company can pay its debts as they fall due.
- [ ] **Proper Accounts:** Maintained proper books of accounts preventing fraud and error.
- [ ] **No Loans to Directors:** ensured the company has NOT provided loans or guarantees to directors or their close relatives (unless explicitly permitted for public companies with banking approval).

## 4. Prohibited Acts (To Avoid Personal Liability)

- [ ] **Misdescription:** The company's full name is clearly displayed on all letters, invoices, and notices. (Personal liability attaches if omitted).
- [ ] **Fraudulent Trading:** The business is not being carried on with the intent to defraud creditors.
- [ ] **Prospectus Misstatement:** (For Public Companies) All statements in the prospectus are true and not misleading.

## 5. Specific to Public Companies

- [ ] **Minimum Directors:** The Board consists of at least 3 directors (max 11).
- [ ] **Independent Director:** (If listed) At least one independent director is appointed.
- [ ] **Cooling Off:** Ensuring the auditor appointed has not worked as an employee/partner in the last 3 years.

::: warning Liability Alert
A director who contravenes these duties may be personally liable for any loss suffered by the company and may face fines up to Rs. 50,000 or imprisonment up to 2 years under Section 160 of the Companies Act.
:::
